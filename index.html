In this module, we will learn about the concept of Word2Vec as a word-embedding model, as well as look at the ways that Word2Vec could be applied to the real world.
1. What is Word2Vec?
Word2Vec is a method of producing word-embeddings that involve training a large corpus of text data and transforming it into a set of vectors. That's right - vectors as in the ones you see in coordinates and spaces. How do these apply to text and words, a distinctly abstract form of information?

2. How are the vectors created?
Breaking the code down into parameters
There are two main methods that Word2Vec uses to predict a certain target word based on its neighboring words. The first method is called the CBOW (Continuous-Bag-of-Words) model, and the second is called the Skip-Gram Model.
The CBOW model predicts a word based on the sum of its neighboring words, and the Skip-Gram model does the exact opposite, predicting neighboring words from a single word.
